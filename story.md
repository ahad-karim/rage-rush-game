Story before pic 2
Time slowed down. The roar of the train was deafening—a wall of blue and yellow steel
screaming across the tracks. The bus felt like paper. Shattering glass, the smell of burning oil,
and a crushing weight. That was the last thing I remember.

Story before pic 3
Cold. That’s what woke me up. I wasn't on the bus anymore. I wasn't on the tracks. I opened my
eyes to a ceiling of rusted pipes and shadows. My head throbbed, and the air tasted like copper
and old machinery.
"Where am I...?"
I’m alive, but I’m not home. I don't know this place, but the heavy iron door in front of me is the
only way out. It’s time to move.
